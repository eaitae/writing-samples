Ceramic tile is one of the few flooring materials that truly works in any room of the house. It’s most often used in kitchens and bathrooms as well as foyers, mudrooms, and other high-traffic areas. You really can’t go wrong with tile. If there are any drawbacks to this durable, elegant flooring, it is that it can be hard and is somewhat tricky for DIYers to install. Ceramic tiles have a hard surface that does not hold onto dirt, dust, pollen, or other allergens. When these small particles do land on a ceramic floor, they stand out against the surface, making it easy to wipe them away with a mop or sponge. We could describe ceramic tiles in three words: durable, versatile and resistant, so if you are looking for those qualities in a floor, ceramic tile is the right choice for you.
